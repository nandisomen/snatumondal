Experimental particle Physicist at CERN - ATLAS collaboration

Particle physics. Top quarks. ATLAS.

I am a physicist at CERN and Czech Technical University in Prague, working within the ATLAS experiment. I focus on top-quark production in high-energy proton–proton and heavy-ion collisions, contributing to cross-section measurements, statistical analysis, and calibration frameworks for b-jet tagging.

Physics Analysis Experience

Observation of top‑quark pair production in lead‑lead collisions

The combined 2015 and 2018 Pb‑Pb collision data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV are used to study $t\bar{t}$ production, with top quarks reconstructed in the $e\mu$ dilepton channel. This analysis, utilising a total integrated luminosity of $1.9\ \mathrm{fb}^{-1}$, marks the first observation of $t\bar{t}$ production in Pb‑Pb collisions with the ATLAS experiment. The measured cross‑section is $\sigma_{t\bar{t}} = 3.6^{+1.0}_{-0.9}\ \text{(stat.)}\ ^{+0.8}_{-0.5}\ \text{(syst.)}\ \mu\text{b}$. This result highlights the ATLAS detector’s ability to probe rare processes in heavy‑ion collisions, offering new insights into the interaction between the top quark and the quark‑gluon plasma.

Search for $t\bar{t}H$ production in multilepton final states using $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 13$ TeV with an integrated luminosity of $80\ \text{fb}^{-1}$. Six final states, categorised by charged‑lepton number and flavour, were defined, along with 25 event categories, to simultaneously detect the $t\bar{t}H$ signal and constrain significant backgrounds. An excess of events consistent with $t\bar{t}H$ production was observed, corresponding to 1.8 standard deviations, compared to the expected 3.1 standard deviations. Assuming Standard Model branching fractions, the best‑fit $t\bar{t}H$ production cross section is $\sigma_{t\bar{t}H} = 294^{+182}_{-162}\ \text{fb}$, which is consistent with Standard Model predictions.

ATLAS Flavour tagging: jet collection calibration and CDI tooling
Read More

I developed and maintained the pseudo‑continuous calibration framework for flavour tagging and b‑jet identification in ATLAS simulations and data. Using the full Run 2 dataset (139 fb−1 at √s = 13 TeV), I established a robust methodology for extracting b‑tagging efficiency and c‑/light‑jet misidentification rates directly from data. To support validation and integration workflows, I created tools to visualise calibrations directly from CDI files, enabling fast cross‑release comparisons and reducing redundancy. These tools streamlined the calibration pipeline and have been adopted across multiple ATLAS analyses requiring precise flavour tagging performance.
